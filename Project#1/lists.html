Definition list

Canadian cheeses

Sauvagine
La Sauvagine is a soft, surface-ripened and washed-rind cheese made from pasteurized cow's milk.
The cheese is named after the 13 million ducks, geese and teals that cloud the Quebec sky in the fall. These migratory birds are collectively called Sauvagine in French.
Cheese Curds
Cheese curds are bite-size solid parts of soured milk either eaten alone or used in regional dishes, especially in Canada and United States.
Cheese curds form the main ingredient in Poutine, a Canadian dish made with French fries, topped with a light brown gravy-like sauce and Cheese curds.
Météorite
Météorite is a soft, surface-ripened, ash-covered blue cheese made from pasteurized cow's milk and cream.
